7 · Get agents involved (recommended for FSBO)

Offering a buyer's-agent commission is one of the best moves a for-sale-by-owner can make.

Why do it: most buyers work with an agent, and agents steer their clients to properties that pay a commission. Offer one and you put 3545 in front of every agent's qualified, ready buyers — not just people browsing online.

  • More traffic, real buyers — agents bring pre-qualified clients
  • You still save money — you only pay the buyer's side, not the listing side, so roughly half of a traditional 5–6% total
  • You keep control — it's still your sale; the agent represents their buyer

Default offer: 2.5%, paid at closing to the agent who brings the buyer. Adjust your % anytime — it updates your agent brochure automatically. Open the agent brochure →

9 · Responsiveness & staying safe

Fast, careful replies sell faster — and keep the scammers out.

Reply fast

  • Answer inquiries within a few hours — interest cools quickly
  • Keep a saved reply ready: the link plus a line inviting questions
  • Always point people to 3545UniformSt.com for the full story

Protect yourself from scammers & hucksters

  • Never share bank info, SSN, or wire money to "verify" anything
  • Be wary of anyone who won't talk by phone, overpays, or rushes a deposit
  • Ignore "agents" who promise a buyer for an upfront fee
  • Never accept a check for more than the price (classic overpayment scam)
  • Take earnest money only through a title company or real estate attorney
  • Meet serious buyers in person and bring someone with you to showings
  • Trust your gut — if it feels off, slow down and verify first
